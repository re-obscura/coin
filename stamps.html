How do we grade stamps?

Grading stamps is an intricate process that combines both art and science, requiring a deep understanding of philately.

Centering

The symmetry of the design within perforations. "Superb" for perfectly centered, down to "Average".

Perforations

Checking for full, intact, and uniformly shaped holes. Irregularities can lower value.

Color

Authentic colors should be vibrant. Fading or discoloration results in a lower grade.

Gum

Original, undisturbed gum is premium. Redistributed or damaged gum lowers the grade.

Cancellations

Light cancellations are preferred for used stamps. Heavy "killers" detract from value.

Standards

We aim to provide a clear, consistent, and fair measure of a stamp’s quality.
