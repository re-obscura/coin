How do we grade gold and silver in Winchester, VA?

Valuing precious metals requires careful analysis of purity, weight, condition, and rarity. Understanding this process is important for making informed decisions.

1

Purity of the Metal

Measuring fineness (karats/percentages). Testing with traditional assaying or modern XRF for fast results.

2

Determining Weight

Weighing in troy ounces. Accuracy is extremely important as minor discrepancies affect price.

3

Item Condition

Critical for coins and jewelry. Using Sheldon system (1-70) for coins. Visual/microscopic inspection for jewelry.

4

Rarity & History

Rare or historically significant coins are worth more. Unusual designs, minting errors, and provenance impact value.

5

Certification

Certification by PCGS/NGC increases credibility. Provides authoritative information on authenticity and quality.

6

Fair Value

Combining weight, purity, condition, and rarity data helps determine the value accurately.
