Preparation Advice

Before contacting Fairfax Coin and Bullion Exchange, you should take a detailed inventory of your coins. Collect all your coins and sort them by type, year of issue, condition and other important characteristics. It is convenient to use catalog sheets or specialized coin inventory programs for this purpose.

Cleaning Warning: Before you turn in your coins, make sure they are properly cleaned and packaged. However, be careful: not all coins should be cleaned, as this can damage their surface and reduce their value. If you are unsure, keep your coins in their original condition.

If your coins are stored in placers, sort them by type in bags. Those of you who have already put your coins in a flip, folder, slab or other holder, please bring them in that way.

How do we grade coins?

Condition

The degree of wear and tear. Less wear equals higher value.

Scarcity

Years and mints that produced fewer coins ("best dates").

Content

The material from which the coin is made (Gold, Silver, etc.).

Demand

How popular the specific series is among collectors.

When grading coins in Fairfax, VA, we perform a Physical Inspection for wear and scratches, assess minting quality, and perform Authentication. After these steps, the coin is given a numerical grade reflecting its overall condition and historical value.
