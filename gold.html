How do we grade gold?

Grading gold is eventful as it is a very complex process, which is a combination of exact science and creativity. Several factors play out to give accurate representation and worth.

Purity (Carats)

Classified by carats (24k is pure). 18k (75%) and 14k (58.3%) are common in jewelry, alloyed for strength.

Weight

Expressed in troy ounces (31.1 grams). Weight is the actual measure of gold content.

Craftsmanship

Design intricacy, workmanship quality, and precision of additional elements like gemstones.

Market Demands: Economic factors, geopolitical events, and consumer trends influence value. Prices often rise during economic instability.

Certification & Hallmarking: Certified pieces come with documentation verifying purity. Hallmarking involves stamping items with marks indicating purity, manufacturer, and assay office.

Methods of Gold Authentication

Authentication is an important process for the jewelry industry, investors, and collectors.

Visual & Weight

Checking for grades (585, 750, 999). Gold is heavy (sg ~19.32 g/cm³); light items may be fake.

Magnetic Test

Gold is not magnetic. If it attracts to a strong magnet, it's an alloy with magnetic metals.

Acid Test

A reliable method. Reaction to specialized acid indicates purity. No reaction means authentic gold.

Spectral Analysis

Extremely precise X-ray fluorescence (XRF) analysis determines exact alloy composition.

Electronic Testers

Handheld devices verify conductivity to differentiate genuine gold from counterfeits.

Scratch Test

"Rock test". Scratching an inconspicuous spot to reveal if there's base metal under plating.

Lab Testing

Time-consuming but most accurate. Determines purity, history, and possible fakes.

Density Test

Measuring water displacement vs weight to calculate density and determine composition.
